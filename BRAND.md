# MercuriL Brand Kit — v1.0 (2026-07-05)

One system across the website, the pitch deck, and every document a judge or
council officer touches. Visual reference: the deployed site. If a slide and
the site disagree, the site wins.

## Name

**MercuriL** — capital M, capital L, always. Never "Mercuril", "MERCURIL",
or "mercuril" in prose.

## Logo

Bar + wordmark: vertical cyan-gradient bar (`#38BDF8 → #0EA5E9`, radius 2px,
subtle glow) + "MercuriL" in Plus Jakarta Sans Bold. Clearspace = bar height.
Wordmark in Ink `#E8EEF6` on dark, `#0B1524` on paper. No taglines beside the
mark. No improvised icons.

Proportion: ground ~88%, panel ~9%, cyan ~2.5%, amber <1%. **One cyan moment
per slide.** Depth = single radial glow (`rgba(56,189,248,.13)` ellipse
bleeding off an edge), title/closing slides only. Never linear two-colour
gradient fills.

**Paper variant** (printed docs, LOIs, grants): white ground, text `#0B1524`,
accent `#0EA5E9` (not #38BDF8 — fails contrast on white), rules `#D8E0E8`.
Decks stay dark.

## Voice

Engineer explaining a life-safety system: calm, specific, zero hype. Short
sentences, concrete nouns. Emotional weight from facts, not adjectives.

Phrase bank:
- "No one should drown because the map was wrong."
- "The road is flooded. The map says drive."
- "Verified ground truth, delivered."
- "the crossings nobody monitors"
- "You keep decision authority. We keep the data honest."

Load-bearing words: verified, ground truth, duty of care, trafficability.
Banned: revolutionary, disrupting, AI-powered, world-class, game-changing,
seamless. No exclamation marks.

## Signature components

- `// Kicker //` opens every section/slide — the brand's signature tic
- Status line: amber dot + `PROTOTYPE PHASE` in DM Mono caps (honest state only)
- Stat block: DM Mono number (cyan em), Slate caption
- Coordinates sign-off: `33.9173°S, 151.2313°E · SYDNEY NSW`
- Footer disclaimer: "MercuriL provides monitoring and alerting data. Road
  authorities, councils and emergency services maintain full decision
  authority for all safety and operational decisions."

## Deck recipe (Canva 16:9)

- Background flat Night Road; radial glow on title + closing only
- Title slide: mark top-left, kicker, one two-line PJS-700 headline,
  status line bottom-left, coordinates bottom-right — nothing else
- Kicker top-left of every content slide: `// 01 — Problem //` numbered to
  match the judging-criteria one-heading-per-slide rule
- Charts: series 1 Current, series 2 Slate, hazard marker Amber; faint grid
  `rgba(255,255,255,.07)`; no 3D; ≤2 series → label lines directly, no legend
- Photos: documentary only (real crossings, floodwater, field hardware),
  duotone toward navy, saturation −40%. No stock people.
- Diagrams: Panel nodes, 1px light borders, DM Mono labels, cyan glow only on
  the node that matters (the aggregator)

## Rubric guardrails (Burt doctrine — deck, site, any public artifact)

No pricing tables, no "buy now", no revenue framing (ARR/MRR/"customers"),
no ABN or registration, no press-release/launch language. Ceiling framing:
"in prototype phase, incubated with UNSW, forming an expert bench." Paid
validation (LOIs, signed pilot commitments) is the strongest thing shown.
