MercuriL

The map that sees the flood.

On New Year's Eve 2025, a father and daughter followed their navigation app onto a flooded road near Darwin. They escaped through the car window and walked 13 km to safety. The map never knew the road was gone.

87% of fatal vehicle flood incidents happen at crossings — causeways, bridges, dips. Nobody is standing there at 2am to report them.

MercuriL is a network of low-cost satellite-IoT depth sensors at exactly those crossings, feeding verified, real-time closures into the maps people already trust.

What's real in this prototype

  • Real: every grey dot — NSW closures/hazards from the federal Roadworks & Road Closures dataset (refreshed daily), and routing on the real road network.
  • Simulated: the sensors. The hardware is in prototyping — this shows the network exactly as it would behave.
