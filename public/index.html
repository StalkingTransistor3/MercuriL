// Problem //

The road is flooded. The map says drive.

Navigation apps rarely show timely, verified flood closures — especially in rural Australia. Under their terms of service, the platforms disclaim duty of care for third-party data integrity, which limits what they can safely ingest.

Road authorities and emergency services do carry that duty. But their closure data is fragmented across states, agencies and platforms, arrives late, and rarely reaches the driver in time. Predictive flood models infer conditions from rainfall, gauges and terrain — but gauges fail during the exact events that matter, and inference cannot tell you when a road is safe to reopen.

The result is a pattern Australians know too well: cars entering water at unmonitored rural crossings, avoidable strandings, and roads left closed longer than they need to be.

// Solution //

Verified ground truth, delivered.

MercuriL pairs low-cost detection modules at flood-prone crossings with a verified-closure feed that unifies official sources and ground-truth data.

The detection module

Ruggedised, pole-mounted sensor modules deployed at flood-prone rural crossings. Each module measures water depth and velocity against UNSW Water Research Lab trafficability criteria — the same thresholds councils and emergency services use to decide whether a vehicle can safely cross.

  • Target unit cost: AUD $50 – $150 per module
  • NB-IoT + geostationary satellite comms — works with no mobile coverage
  • Estimated satellite data cost: under AUD $5 / month / device
  • IP67-rated, engineered for years of unattended field service

The verified-closure feed

A middleware pipeline that aggregates official sources — Bureau of Meteorology, state Departments of Transport, State Emergency Services — and overlays sensor ground-truth into a single, verified closure feed.

  • One canonical feed per state, harmonised nationally
  • Available to navigation platforms under an integrity-warranted API
  • Emergency-ops view for council + SES operators
  • Ground-truth reopening signal — not just closure inference

// How it works //

From unmonitored crossing to verified feed.

01 — WE MAP

Find the gaps

We work with councils to identify the critical crossings — where the water rises, where the data isn't, and where the closure signal never reaches a driver.

02 — WE SENSE

Deploy the modules

Detection modules installed at the crossings nobody monitors. Depth and velocity, measured against WRL trafficability thresholds, in real time.

03 — WE VERIFY

One trusted feed

Official closures and sensor ground-truth, aggregated into a single verified feed — pushed to navigation platforms and emergency operations.

04 — YOU DECIDE

Authority stays with you

The data and alerts are yours to act on. Councils and emergency services keep full decision authority over closures, reopenings and public-safety responses.
